# VCM-704LAB-UET

## Video Coding for Machine Vision Research Group

**VCM-704LAB-UET** is a research group at the University of Engineering and Technology, Vietnam National University, Hanoi, focusing on efficient video coding systems for machine vision applications.

Our research addresses a fundamental shift in modern multimedia systems: video is no longer consumed only by humans, but also by intelligent machines such as object detectors, segmenters, trackers, autonomous agents, robots, and edge analytics systems.

## Why Video Coding for Machines?

Conventional video coding standards are mainly optimized for human visual quality. However, many modern applications transmit videos primarily for machine analysis. In such scenarios, preserving task-relevant information may be more important than maximizing perceptual quality alone.

We study how to encode videos more intelligently by considering:

- machine vision accuracy,
- bitrate and bandwidth constraints,
- latency and edge deployment cost,
- region-level semantic importance,
- compatibility with existing video coding standards,
- human-machine quality trade-offs.

## Main Research Directions

| Direction | Research Focus |
|---|---|
| ROI-aware Video Coding | Task-aware bit allocation for important visual regions |
| Compression-domain Vision | Exploiting motion vectors, residuals, coding modes, and transform information |
| Edge-oriented VCM | Real-time coding and analytics on embedded/edge platforms |
| Neural Preprocessing | Learning-based preprocessing before standard video codecs |
| Benchmarking and Evaluation | Reproducible protocols for evaluating machine-oriented coding systems |

---

## Research Philosophy

We follow three principles:

1. **Scientific Rigor**: Every proposed method must be connected to a clear research question, baseline, experimental protocol, and measurable contribution.
2. **Reproducibility**: Code, configuration, datasets, and evaluation scripts should be organized for independent verification.
3. **Practical Relevance**: Methods should be compatible with real video coding systems and useful for intelligent applications.

# About VCM-704LAB-UET

**VCM-704LAB-UET** is a research group working on video coding, machine vision, and edge intelligence. The group focuses on developing efficient video compression techniques for intelligent systems where videos are processed by both humans and machines.

## Research Motivation

The rapid growth of intelligent video applications has created new requirements for video coding systems. In many scenarios, video streams are transmitted to support downstream tasks such as object detection, semantic segmentation, multi-object tracking, event recognition, autonomous navigation, and inspection.

This motivates a new research paradigm: **Video Coding for Machines (VCM)**.

Instead of optimizing only for human perceptual quality, VCM investigates how to preserve task-relevant visual information under constraints such as bitrate, latency, energy, and computational complexity.

## Lab Vision

Our long-term vision is to develop a scientific and reproducible research platform for task-aware video coding, bridging the gap between standard video compression and modern machine vision.

We aim to contribute to:

- new video coding algorithms for machine vision,
- standard-compatible optimization methods,
- edge-oriented visual analytics pipelines,
- task-aware evaluation protocols,
- open-source tools and benchmarks,
- high-quality academic publications.

## Core Research Questions

1. How should video coding systems allocate bits when the final consumer is a machine vision model?
2. Which visual regions, temporal segments, or compressed-domain features are most important for downstream tasks?
3. How can standard codecs such as HEVC and VVC be adapted without breaking compatibility?
4. How can edge devices perform video coding and analytics under limited resources?
5. How should we evaluate video coding systems when both human and machine requirements exist?

## Working Style

Our group follows a structured research workflow:

- reading and summarizing related work,
- identifying research gaps,
- building reproducible baselines,
- proposing methodologically clear improvements,
- running controlled experiments,
- analyzing results scientifically,
- preparing manuscripts, slides, posters, and technical reports.

## Research Outputs

Expected outputs include:

- journal papers,
- conference papers,
- open-source repositories,
- benchmark protocols,
- student theses,
- research demonstrations,
- technical reports and tutorials.
